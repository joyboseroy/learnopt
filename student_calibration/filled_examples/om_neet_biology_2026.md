## What this is

We've now run real analysis on ~1500 actual NEET questions from 2016-2024.
Found something genuinely interesting: NEET's question-style changed
noticeably around 2023 (likely tied to the NCERT syllabus reduction that
year) -- fewer pure-recall questions, more "which of these is INCORRECT"
style questions. Your input turns this from a data analysis into something
that's actually validated by someone who lived through NEET prep.

## Task 3: Label 10 real NEET questions (20 min)

These are REAL questions from NEET 2018 and NEET 2023 papers (5 each).
For each, just say what kind of thinking it needs. Pick ONE:

- **Recall** -- you either know the fact or you don't, no thinking needed
- **Apply** -- you know the idea, but have to use it in a new situation
- **Combine** -- you need TWO different topics/chapters together to answer
- **Calculate** -- needs maths/numbers
- **Eliminate** -- question says "NOT" or "EXCEPT", or gives statements/
  assertion-reason pairs to verify

**Q1 (2018).** In which disease does a mosquito-transmitted pathogen cause
chronic inflammation of lymphatic vessels?
(a) Ringworm disease (b) Ascariasis (c) Elephantiasis (d) Amoebiasis

**Q2 (2018).** Select the correct statement:
(a) Spliceosomes take part in translation.
(b) The Punnett square was developed by a British scientist.
(c) Franklin and Stahl coined the term "linkage".
(d) Transduction was discovered by S. Altman.

**Q3 (2018).** Pollen grains can be stored for several years in liquid
nitrogen having a temperature of:
(a) -196°C (b) -80°C (c) -120°C (d) -160°C

**Q4 (2018).** Pneumatophores occur in:
(a) Carnivorous plants (b) Free-floating hydrophytes
(c) Halophytes (d) Submerged hydrophytes

**Q5 (2018).** Stomatal movement is NOT affected by:
(a) O2 concentration (b) Light (c) Temperature (d) CO2 concentration

**Q6 (2023).** Identify the correct statements:
A. Detritivores perform fragmentation.
B. Humus is further degraded by some microbes during mineralization.
C. Water-soluble inorganic nutrients go down into the soil and get
   precipitated by a process called leaching.
D. The detritus food chain begins with living organisms.
E. Earthworms break down detritus into smaller particles by a process
   called catabolism.
(a) D, E, A only (b) A, B, C only (c) B, C, D only (d) C, D, E only

**Q7 (2023).** In the equation GPP - R = NPP, where GPP is Gross Primary
Productivity and NPP is Net Primary Productivity, R here is:
(a) Reproductive allocation (b) Photosynthetically active radiation
(c) Respiratory quotient (d) Respiratory loss

**Q8 (2023).** The parts of the human brain that help in regulation of

(a) Corpus callosum and thalamus (b) Limbic system and hypothalamus
(c) Corpora quadrigemina and hippocampus (d) Brain stem and epithalamus

**Q9 (2023).** Assertion A: ATP is used at two steps in glycolysis.

(a) A is false but R is true.
(b) Both A and R are true and R is the correct explanation of A.
(c) Both A and R are true but R is NOT the correct explanation of A.
(d) A is true but R is false.

**Q10 (2023).** Statement I: During the G0 phase of the cell cycle, the

Statement II: The centrosome undergoes duplication during the S phase of

(a) Statement I is incorrect but Statement II is correct.
(b) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct.
(c) Both Statement I and Statement II are incorrect.
(d) Statement I is correct but Statement II is incorrect.

## Task 4: The 2023 shift -- does this match your experience? (10 min)

Our analysis found that NEET 2023-2024 questions have noticeably FEWER pure
recall/definition questions and noticeably MORE "which statement is
INCORRECT / all of the following EXCEPT / Assertion-Reason / Statement I &
Statement II" style questions, compared to 2016-2021. (You'll have seen this
directly in Q9 and Q10 above -- that Assertion-Reason and twin-statement
format barely shows up in our 2018 sample but is common in 2023.) This seems
to line up with NCERT removing several chapters from the syllabus in 2023.

Does this match what you've heard from seniors / teachers / your own sense
of recent papers vs older papers -- specifically, does the Assertion-Reason /
twin-statement format feel like something that's become MORE common
recently? One paragraph, totally honest -- even "never noticed this" is a
useful answer.
